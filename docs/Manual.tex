\section{Introduction}
% =========================================================================

\texttt{modenanalyse\_2fe2s} is a Python package for mode-resolved
bonding reorganization analysis of [2Fe-2S] iron-sulfur clusters from
DFT frequency calculations. For each vibrational mode and each
relevant bonding channel, it computes thermally scaled bond
modulations $\Delta r_X$ and the associated Marcus-Hush
reorganization contributions $\lambda_X$ in a form directly
comparable to spectroscopic observables (NRVS, NIS).

\subsection{What the tool does}

The pipeline takes a Gaussian-16 \texttt{.log} or ORCA-6 \texttt{.hess}
frequency calculation of a [2Fe-2S]-containing protein region (and
optionally a PDB file for ligand identification) and produces:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Per-mode analysis}: Eigenvector-based classification
    of all normal modes into in-plane / out-of-plane / torsional
    based on the cluster plane.
  \item \textbf{Reorganization energies} per bonding channel
    $X \in \{\text{Fe-Fe}, \text{Fe-S}, \text{Fe-N}, \text{N-H},
    \text{H-acceptor}\}$ and per mode, both as system totals and
    per-bond resolved.
  \item \textbf{Frequency-resolved modulation spectra} $M_X(\omega)$
    suitable for direct comparison with NRVS / NIS measurements.
  \item \textbf{Cumulative reorganization} $\Lambda_X(\omega)$ for
    identifying which spectral regions dominate.
  \item \textbf{SCSD symmetry-coordinate decomposition} of the
    cluster core into $D_{2h}$ irreps (after Kingsbury \& Senge,
    2024).
  \item \textbf{UMAP embeddings} of the 31-feature mode space with
    HDBSCAN clustering for mode-landscape exploration.
  \item \textbf{Multi-cluster support}: dimers and multi-[2Fe-2S]
    systems are handled as a first-class feature.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Target users}

The intended users are researchers in biophysical chemistry,
bioinorganic chemistry, and quantum chemistry who study iron-sulfur
proteins. Typical applications include:

\begin{itemize}
  \item NRVS band assignment in Rieske-type proteins, mitoNEET, and
    ferredoxins.
  \item Comparative reorganization-energy studies between mutants
    or oxidation states.
  \item Identification of vibrational modes relevant to PCET.
  \item Validation of QM/MM models against vibrational data.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Scope and limitations}

The tool assumes a single [2Fe-2S] cluster (or multiple, with
\texttt{analyze\_all\_clusters=True}) per input system. It does not
currently handle [4Fe-4S], [3Fe-4S], or larger iron-sulfur
clusters --- these would require different cluster-detection logic
and different SCSD reference geometries.

The reorganization formalism is local-mode based: each individual
bond is treated independently via the Marcus-Hush expression
$\lambda = \tfrac{1}{2}\mu\omega^2 (\Delta r)^2$. Couplings between
bonding channels are not currently included beyond what is implicit
in the DFT normal modes themselves.

\section{Theoretical Background}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{The Marcus-Hush reorganization energy}

In Marcus electron-transfer theory, the reorganization energy
$\lambda$ measures the energy cost of distorting the equilibrium
geometry of one electronic state to match the equilibrium geometry of
another. For inner-sphere reorganization in a single bond at
frequency $\omega$ with reduced mass $\mu$ and bond-length change
$\Delta r$, the harmonic approximation gives

\begin{equation}
\lambda = \tfrac{1}{2}\, \mu\, \omega^2\, (\Delta r)^2 .
\label{eq:marcus-bond}
\end{equation}

For a multi-mode system, the total reorganization is the sum of
contributions from individual normal modes:

\begin{equation}
\Lambda_X = \sum_i \lambda_X(i),
\label{eq:lambda-total}
\end{equation}

where the sum runs over normal modes $i$ and $X$ is a bonding channel
(Fe-Fe, Fe-N, etc.). This is the central observable produced by this
tool, written to the \texttt{Reorg\_total} sheet.

\subsection{Thermal scaling of eigenvectors}

DFT normal modes are normalized; to convert into physical
displacements, we use the quantum-mechanical RMS amplitude of a
harmonic oscillator at temperature $T$:

\begin{equation}
u_\text{rms}^2 = \frac{\hbar}{2 m \omega} \coth\!\left(\frac{\hbar \omega}{2 k_B T}\right) ,
\label{eq:urms}
\end{equation}

where $m$ is the mode reduced mass and $\omega = 2\pi c \tilde\nu$
in SI units. At $T \to 0$, $\coth \to 1$ and $u_\text{rms}^2 \to
\hbar/(2 m \omega)$ (the zero-point amplitude). At high temperature,
$\coth \to 1/x$ and $u_\text{rms}^2 \to k_B T / (m \omega^2)$
(classical equipartition).

For NRVS measurements, $T = 5$~K is the standard low-temperature
condition; $T = 80$~K is also common. Room temperature (300~K) is
relevant only for room-temperature kinetics studies.

\subsection{Bond-channel modulation $\Delta r_X$}

For each mode $i$ and each bond $X = (a, b)$, the bond-length
modulation is computed from the thermally scaled atomic
displacements $\bm{e}_a, \bm{e}_b$:

\begin{equation}
\Delta r_X(i) = (\bm{e}_a - \bm{e}_b) \cdot \hat{r}_{ab} ,
\label{eq:dr}
\end{equation}

where $\hat{r}_{ab}$ is the unit vector along the equilibrium
bond. This is a signed scalar (positive = bond stretching, negative
= bond compression).

For multiple sub-channels (e.g., FeN with two His ligands), the
per-channel reorganization sums over sub-channels:

\begin{equation}
\lambda_X(i) = \tfrac{1}{2}\, \mu\, \omega_i^2\, \sum_k w_k\, [\Delta r_{X,k}(i)]^2 ,
\end{equation}

with weights $w_k$ accounting for symmetry-equivalent sub-channels
(e.g., two equivalent His ligands each contributing $w = 1/2$).

\subsection{Hydrogen-bond reaction coordinate (PCET)}

For the H-acceptor (HA) channel relevant to PCET, the relevant
coordinate is not a single bond length but the projection of the
hydrogen displacement relative to the donor onto the donor-acceptor
axis:

\begin{equation}
\Delta r_\text{HA}(i) = (\bm{e}_H - \bm{e}_\text{donor}) \cdot \hat{r}_\text{donor-acceptor} .
\label{eq:dr-pcet}
\end{equation}

This captures the actual proton-transfer reaction coordinate. A
nonzero $\Lambda_\text{HA}$ indicates PCET capability of the system.

\subsection{Asymptotic limit at $T \to 0$}

In the zero-temperature limit, eq.~\eqref{eq:urms} gives
$u_\text{rms} = \sqrt{\hbar/(2 m \omega)}$, and
eq.~\eqref{eq:marcus-bond} becomes

\begin{equation}
\lambda_X(T \to 0) = \frac{\hbar \omega}{4} \cdot \alpha_X^2 ,
\label{eq:lambda-T0}
\end{equation}

where $\alpha_X = \Delta r_X / u_\text{rms}$ is the dimensionless
mode-bond coupling. For a single atom moving along the bond axis with
amplitude $\alpha = 1$, this simplifies to $\lambda = \omega/4$ (in
$\hbar\omega$ units, so $\omega/4$ in cm$^{-1}$ when frequency is in
cm$^{-1}$). This is the Huang-Rhys form of vibronic coupling and is
the asymptotic check the implementation passes.

\subsection{Choice of reduced mass}

Two conventions are produced:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Pair-reduced mass} $\mu_\text{pair} = m_a m_b / (m_a + m_b)$:
    treats each bond as an isolated diatomic. Useful for direct
    interpretation of bond-vibrational energies.
  \item \textbf{Mode-reduced mass} $\mu_\text{mode}$: the full DFT
    normal-mode reduced mass, which incorporates couplings to other
    atoms. Produced as \texttt{Lambda\_total\_mode\_cm1} in the
    output. \emph{This is the recommended quantity for Marcus-Hush
    comparisons across systems}, as it correctly includes the inertia
    of the actual collective motion.
\end{itemize}

The two conventions agree when the mode is a pure local bond
vibration; they differ when the mode is delocalized across multiple
bonds.

\subsection{D$_{2h}$ symmetry-coordinate decomposition (SCSD)}

The cluster core (4 atoms: 2 Fe + 2 S) has approximate $D_{2h}$
symmetry: the 12 atomic Cartesian degrees of freedom decompose as

\begin{equation}
\Gamma_\text{core} = A_g \oplus B_{1g} \oplus B_{2g} \oplus B_{3g}
                   \oplus A_u \oplus B_{1u} \oplus B_{2u} \oplus B_{3u}
                   \oplus 4 \, T_\text{trans+rot} .
\end{equation}

The SCSD method (Kingsbury \& Senge, 2024) projects the actual
displacement of the cluster core onto a canonical $D_{2h}$ reference
geometry (Fe-Fe = 2.73~\AA, Fe-S = 2.20~\AA, taken as means from
high-resolution Rieske/ferredoxin crystal structures), with axis
convention $x = $ Fe-Fe direction, $y = $ S-S direction,
$z = $ cluster normal. The decomposition is orthogonal in $D_{2h}$
irreps; values are directly comparable between different structures
and oxidation states.

\section{Installation and First Run}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{Installation}

The package ships as a Python wheel:

\begin{lstlisting}
pip install modenanalyse_2fe2s-1.0.0-py3-none-any.whl
\end{lstlisting}

Dependencies are pulled in automatically (numpy, scipy, pandas,
matplotlib, openpyxl, scikit-learn, umap-learn, hdbscan, scsdpy).

Verify installation:

\begin{lstlisting}
modenanalyse-2fe2s --help
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Quick test}

The package ships with a small test [2Fe-2S] cluster:

\begin{lstlisting}
from modenanalyse_2fe2s import Config, run_analysis

cfg = Config(
    log_file   = "tests/data/Cys_2Fe-2S_red3_hpfrq_opt.log.xz",
    output_dir = "./quickstart_results",
    temp_k     = 5.0,
    freq_max   = 800.0,
)
run_analysis(cfg)
\end{lstlisting}

This takes about 1 minute and produces the full output set.

\subsection{Recommended TOML-based workflow}

For reproducibility, prefer TOML configuration files:

\begin{lstlisting}
modenanalyse-2fe2s --write-template my_run.toml
# edit my_run.toml as needed
modenanalyse-2fe2s --config my_run.toml
\end{lstlisting}

The TOML file documents itself and is git-friendly.

\section{Configuration Reference}
% =========================================================================

The \texttt{Config} dataclass has 48 fields. Most have sensible
defaults; only \texttt{log\_file} and \texttt{output\_dir} are
required.

\subsection{Required fields}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{log\_file}: path to Gaussian-16 \texttt{.log} (with
    \texttt{freq=hpmodes}) or ORCA-6 \texttt{.hess}.
  \item \texttt{output\_dir}: target directory; created if absent.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Geometry input}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{pdb\_file} (optional): for ligand-residue
    identification via Kabsch-aligned matching to the QM cluster.
  \item \texttt{pdb\_chain}: chain identifier (default \texttt{"A"};
    empty string \texttt{""} = all chains).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Frequency window}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{freq\_min}, \texttt{freq\_max}: frequency filter in
    cm$^{-1}$. Defaults: \texttt{None} (no limit) and 800.0 (NRVS
    measurement window).
  \item \texttt{freq\_windows}: list of \texttt{(low, high)} tuples
    for multi-window analysis. Each window gets its own subfolder;
    a top-level overall analysis is also produced.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Temperature}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{temp\_k}: sample temperature in Kelvin. Standard
    values: 5 K (cryogenic NRVS), 40 K (helium standard),
    80 K (liquid nitrogen), 300 K (room temperature). Default: 80.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Cluster selection}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{cluster\_index}: 0-based index of the cluster to
    analyze (default 0 = closest Fe-Fe pair). Ignored when
    \texttt{analyze\_all\_clusters=True}.
  \item \texttt{analyze\_all\_clusters}: \texttt{True} to analyze
    all detected [2Fe-2S] clusters, each in its own
    \texttt{cluster\_N/} subfolder, with a top-level
    \texttt{multi\_cluster\_summary.txt}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Module selection}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{analyze\_scsd}: enable SCSD $D_{2h}$ decomposition
    (default \texttt{True}).
  \item \texttt{pcet\_enabled}: enable PCET reorganization pipeline
    (default \texttt{True}).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Numerical parameters}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{sigma\_eigvec}: noise amplitude added to eigenvectors
    for sensitivity analysis (default $5 \times 10^{-4}$).
  \item \texttt{sigma\_coord}: noise amplitude added to atomic
    coordinates (default $1 \times 10^{-3}$).
  \item \texttt{interp\_step}: grid spacing for interpolated spectra
    in cm$^{-1}$ (default 0.05).
  \item \texttt{interp\_boundary\_mode}: \texttt{"context"},
    \texttt{"zero"}, or \texttt{"nearest"} (default
    \texttt{"context"}).
  \item \texttt{amplitude}: fallback amplitude in classical mode
    (\texttt{temp\_k=None}); default 1.0.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{PCET geometry}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{pcet\_acceptor\_r0\_a}: H-bond acceptor reference
    distance (default 2.8~\AA).
  \item \texttt{pcet\_acceptor\_sigma\_a}: weighting sigma
    (default 0.4~\AA).
  \item \texttt{pcet\_hbond\_cutoff\_a}: maximum H-acceptor distance
    for inclusion (default 3.5~\AA).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Output controls}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{include\_hydrogen}: include H atoms in mode analysis
    (default \texttt{True}).
  \item \texttt{use\_cache}: speed up repeated runs on the same log
    file via byte-offset cache (default \texttt{True}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Output Files Reference}
% =========================================================================

\subsection{REPORT.txt}

Human-readable summary, written as \texttt{<basename>\_REPORT.txt}.
Sections (in order): CONFIGURATION, INSTALLED MODULES, GEOMETRY,
COORDINATING AMINO ACIDS, MODE ANALYSIS, MODE DISTRIBUTION,
CLUSTER ANALYSIS, FAILED MODES, WARNINGS, ERRORS, NOTES, OUTPUT FILES.

The NOTES section is the densest with information --- it includes
the headline reorganization values, the SCSD methodology citation,
HP/standard consistency check, and analysis statistics.

\subsection{Main analysis workbook}

Written as \texttt{<basename>\_analysis.xlsx}, with 11 sheets:

\begin{tabular}{ll}
  \toprule
  \textbf{Sheet} & \textbf{Content} \\
  \midrule
  \texttt{Mode\_analysis}         & Per-mode summary (one row per mode) \\
  \texttt{Core\_scores}           & Cluster-core mode scores \\
  \texttt{Equilibrium\_distances} & Fe-Fe, Fe-S, Fe-N, etc. distances \\
  \texttt{SCSD}                   & $D_{2h}$ symmetry decomposition \\
  \texttt{Reorganization\_energy} & Per-mode $\lambda_X(i)$ and $\Delta r_X(i)$ \\
  \texttt{Reorg\_total}           & System totals $\Lambda_X$ \\
  \texttt{Reorg\_per\_bond}       & Per-bond contributions \\
  \texttt{Modulation\_spectra}    & $M_X(\omega)$ \\
  \texttt{Lambda\_cumulative}     & $\Lambda_X(\omega)$ \\
  \texttt{B\_factors}             & Debye-Waller B-factors \\
  \texttt{Info}                   & Run metadata \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsubsection{Mode\_analysis}

One row per analyzed mode. Columns:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{Mode\#}, \texttt{Frequency} (cm$^{-1}$),
    \texttt{u\_rms} (\AA), \texttt{Red.mass} (AMU),
    \texttt{Frc.const.} (mDyn/\AA): from DFT.
  \item \texttt{Sym.}: irrep label.
  \item \texttt{Type}: classification (\texttt{In-plane},
    \texttt{Out-of-plane}, \texttt{Torsional/Mixed}).
  \item \texttt{Type (fine)}: 7-level classification (\texttt{Pure
    OOP}, \texttt{Strong OOP}, \texttt{Majority OOP},
    \texttt{Mixed}, \texttt{Majority INP}, \texttt{Strong INP},
    \texttt{Pure INP}).
  \item \texttt{Prec.}: precision (\texttt{high} for HP eigenvector
    blocks, \texttt{standard} otherwise).
  \item Ring-2 / Ring-3 / Ring-1 columns: percentages of mode amplitude
    on ligand atoms / secondary sphere / cluster core.
  \item \texttt{Lambda\_FeFe}, \texttt{Lambda\_NH},
    \texttt{Lambda\_HA} (cm$^{-1}$): per-mode reorganization
    contributions for the three most informative channels.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Reorg\_total --- the headline numbers}

Five rows, one per channel:

\begin{tabular}{llll}
  \toprule
  \textbf{Channel} & \textbf{$\Lambda$\_pair (cm$^{-1}$)} & \textbf{$\Lambda$\_mode (cm$^{-1}$)} & \textbf{n\_modes} \\
  \midrule
  FeFe & ... & ... & ... \\
  FeN  & ... & ... & ... \\
  FeS  & ... & ... & ... \\
  NH   & ... & ... & ... \\
  HA   & ... & ... & ... \\
  \bottomrule
\end{tabular}

Use \texttt{Lambda\_total\_mode\_cm1} for cross-system comparisons.
A non-zero $\Lambda_\text{HA}$ indicates PCET capability.

\subsubsection{Reorganization\_energy --- per-mode breakdown}

One row per mode, one column block per channel. For each channel
$X$:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{dr\_X\_rss\_a}: root-sum-of-squares of bond
    modulations across sub-channels (signed sum is also available
    as \texttt{dr\_X\_sum\_signed\_a}).
  \item \texttt{lambda\_X\_pair\_cm1}: $\lambda$ with pair-reduced
    mass.
  \item \texttt{lambda\_X\_mode\_cm1}: $\lambda$ with
    mode-reduced mass.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Modulation\_spectra}

Frequency-resolved $M_X(\omega) = \sum_i \delta(\omega - \omega_i)
\cdot |\Delta r_X(i)|^2$ broadened with a Gaussian. Useful for
direct visual comparison with NRVS spectra.

\subsubsection{Lambda\_cumulative}

$\Lambda_X(\omega) = \sum_{i: \omega_i \le \omega} \lambda_X(i)$.
Plotted versus $\omega$, this shows which spectral regions
contribute most to the total reorganization.

\subsection{Embeddings workbook}

\texttt{<basename>\_analysis\_Embeddings.xlsx}: PCA, UMAP, and t-SNE
projections of the 31-feature mode space, with HDBSCAN cluster
labels and per-cluster characterization (Z-scores, Fisher F).

\subsection{Plots}

\texttt{<basename>\_embedding\_UMAP.png}: 2D projection of all modes,
left panel colored by frequency, right panel colored by mode type.

\section{Multi-Cluster Workflow}
% =========================================================================

For dimers (e.g., glutaredoxin) and multi-domain systems with multiple
[2Fe-2S] clusters, set \texttt{analyze\_all\_clusters=True}:

\begin{lstlisting}
cfg = Config(
    log_file   = "dimer.hess",
    pdb_file   = "dimer.pdb",
    output_dir = "./results_dimer",
    analyze_all_clusters = True,
    temp_k = 5.0,
)
run_analysis(cfg)
\end{lstlisting}

The pipeline:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Detects all [2Fe-2S] clusters in the input.
  \item For each cluster, runs the full analysis in a separate
    \texttt{cluster\_N/} subfolder, with cluster-specific Fe-Fe and
    Fe-S identification.
  \item Writes a top-level \texttt{multi\_cluster\_summary.txt} with
    geometry and run status per cluster.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Multi-cluster Kabsch alignment}

For multi-cluster systems, the Kabsch alignment of QM coordinates to
PDB requires care: with $>$2 Fe atoms in the PDB, the algorithm
chooses the Fe pair whose Fe-Fe distance is closest to the QM
Fe-Fe distance \emph{and} is below 4~\AA{} (cluster-internal
distance). This avoids accidental cross-cluster alignment that
would otherwise produce silently wrong ligand assignments.

\subsubsection*{Single-cluster fallback}

For single-cluster systems, \texttt{analyze\_all\_clusters=True}
falls back automatically to single-cluster mode (no subfolders).

\section{Validation}
% =========================================================================

The implementation has been validated against:

\subsection{Numerical formula verification}

All core formulas were independently re-implemented and checked to
six decimal places:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Eq.~\eqref{eq:urms} (thermal amplitude): zero-point limit
    matches at $T \to 0$, classical limit converges at $T \to \infty$.
  \item Eq.~\eqref{eq:marcus-bond} (Marcus-Hush): $T \to 0$ with
    $\alpha = 1$ gives $\lambda = \omega/4$ exactly.
  \item Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambda-T0} (low-T asymptotic): consistency
    check verified at multiple frequencies and reduced masses.
  \item Kabsch SVD: standard reflection-corrected variant, verified
    against a known reference.
\end{itemize}

  $\Lambda_\text{NH}$ (mode)   & 0 \\

\section{Troubleshooting}
% =========================================================================

\subsection*{``No [2Fe-2S] cluster found''}

Check that the input contains atomic coordinates. For Gaussian
\texttt{.log}, the file must have a Standard orientation block. For
ORCA \texttt{.hess}, the \texttt{\$atoms} block must be present and
non-empty.

If the cluster has unusually large Fe-Fe (>3.5~\AA) or Fe-S
(>2.7~\AA) distances, increase the cutoffs:

\begin{lstlisting}
cfg = Config(..., fe_fe_cutoff=4.0, fe_s_cutoff=3.0)
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection*{``Kabsch alignment failed''}

Common causes:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Wrong chain identifier. Default is \texttt{"A"}; if your PDB
    uses a different chain, set \texttt{pdb\_chain="X"} or
    \texttt{pdb\_chain=""} (all chains).
  \item PDB has fewer than 2 Fe or 2 S atoms.
  \item PDB and QM coordinates are in completely different reference
    frames and the Fe-Fe distance differs by more than the algorithm
    can tolerate.
\end{itemize}

The REPORT shows the Kabsch RMSD; values $<$ 0.1~\AA{} indicate a
good alignment.

\subsection*{``$\Lambda_\text{NH}$ and $\Lambda_\text{HA}$ are zero
on a Rieske protein''}

Check:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item PDB has the His ligands present.
  \item PDB is correctly aligned to the QM region (Kabsch RMSD in
    REPORT).
  \item \texttt{pcet\_enabled=True} (the default).
  \item The His residues' protonation: the tool checks for H on the
    ring nitrogen.
\end{enumerate}

If His ligands are present but \texttt{Lambda\_HA} is zero, the
WARNINGS section will indicate that no H-bond acceptors were found
within the cutoff distance --- typically meaning no nearby protein
groups can act as proton acceptor.

\subsection*{``openpyxl missing''}

\texttt{pip install openpyxl}. Required for Excel output. Pinned in
the package dependencies but may need refresh after Python upgrades.

\subsection*{Slow runs on large systems}

For systems with $>$1500 modes, the per-mode analysis is the
bottleneck. The bottleneck scales linearly with $N_\text{modes}$. A
typical 422-atom system with 1266 modes runs in $\approx$5 minutes
on modern hardware. Use \texttt{use\_cache=True} (default) to speed
up repeated runs on the same input.
